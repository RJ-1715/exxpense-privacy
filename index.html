Exxpense — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 13 June 2026

Exxpense ("the app", "we") is an automatic expense tracker for Android. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected. It applies to the app published under the package name com.exxpense.

SMS data and how we use it

Exxpense reads incoming bank and UPI transaction SMS on your device to automatically detect financial transactions. SMS messages are processed entirely on your device to extract transaction details such as the amount, the merchant, the last four digits of the account, and the date. The full text of your SMS is never transmitted off your device, never shared with third parties, and never sold.

The app reads only bank and UPI transaction alerts. One-time passwords (OTPs), personal messages, and any SMS that is not a bank transaction are ignored and are not turned into transactions.

What we collect and store

In this version, all data — the SMS the app reads and the transactions it creates — is stored locally on your device only. We do not operate a server that receives your SMS or transaction data, and we do not upload, sync, or back up this data anywhere. We do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking services.

Permissions we request

  • Receive SMS / Read SMS — used solely to detect bank transaction messages and create transactions from them. This is the core function of the app; without it the app cannot work. We ask for this permission with a clear on-screen explanation before the Android system prompt appears.

Data sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer your SMS messages or transaction data to any third party. No data leaves your device.

Data retention and deletion

Because all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it at any time by clearing the app's data or uninstalling the app. Uninstalling Exxpense permanently removes all transaction data the app has stored.

Children

Exxpense is not directed to children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices change in a future version — for example, if optional cloud sync is added — this policy will be updated before that version is released, and the change will be reflected in the app's Play Store Data Safety section.
